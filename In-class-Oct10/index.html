Proper livestock housing and welfare significantly improve animal health and well-being. Animals in clean, safe, and comfortable conditions are less likely to undergo stress, illness, or injury. This can of course results in higher growth rates, reproduction, and overall productivity.

The goat is among the cleanest of animals, and is a much more selective feeder than cows, sheep, pigs, chickens and even dogs. Goats do eat many different species of plants, but do not want to eat food that has been contaminated or that has been on the floor or the ground.
